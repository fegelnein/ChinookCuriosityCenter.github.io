Chinook Curiosity Central Mathematical Challenge:

Math contests have long suffered from the misconception that they are tedious and exclusively tailored for a select group of "math geniuses," inadvertently deterring potential participants from exploring the intriguing world of competitive mathematics. Our primary objective is to shatter this stereotype by creating a contest that retains its competitive and rigorous nature while ensuring an enjoyable experience for students, thus fostering their ongoing participation in math competitions and nurturing their passion for the subject.

To achieve this objective, our contest has a novel approach: dividing the contest into three distinctive sessions, each designed to engage and challenge participants in a different way.

Session 1: The Quick Computational Challenge (25 minutes)

In the first session, participants will answer a rapid-fire round of 150 questions as fast as they can within 25 minutes. Each correct answer will earn them one point. While the completion of all questions is a near-impossible feat, this session promises an adrenaline rush of mathematical problem-solving. The top-scoring participants will receive gold, silver, and bronze medals, accompanied by enticing cash prizes.

- Gold Medal: Highest point scorer

- Silver Medals: Two participants with the second-highest scores

- Bronze Medals: Four participants with the third-highest scores

Session 2: The Progressive Challenge (65 minutes)

Building upon the tradition of conventional math contests, this session comprises 15 questions of increasing complexity. The questions are categorized into three tiers: the first five questions are worth 2 points each, the next five are worth 3 points each, and the final five carry a weight of 5 points each. Participants will have 65 minutes to tackle this session, vying for gold, silver, and bronze medals, accompanied by enticing cash prizes.

- Gold Medal: Highest point scorer

- Silver Medals: Two participants with the second-highest scores

- Bronze Medals: Four participants with the third-highest scores

Session 3: The Team Relay Challenge (80 minutes)

This collaborative and interactive session is likely to resonate strongly with our participants. Divided into ten teams, each consisting of 10 members, and then divide each team into two groups of 5. This session introduces a team relay format. In each of the three rounds, participants must work together to correctly answer questions, passing the correct numeric answer from one team member to the next. The objective is to relay the correct answer to a volunteer on the side as swiftly as possible.

Points will be awarded as follows:

First Group: 100 points

Second Group: 60 points

Third Group: 40 points

Fourth Group: 30 points

Fifth Group: 20 points

Sixth to Twentieth Groups: 10 points

Following these rounds, the two groups of 5 will combine to form a "super-relay" of 10 participants in a single group. Points for the super-relay will be distributed as follows:

First Place: 200 points

Second Place: 120 points

Third Place: 80 points

Fourth Place: 60 points

Fifth Place: 50 points

Sixth to Tenth Place: 30 points

The team accumulating the most points throughout the regular relays and the super relay will be crowned the ultimate winner, earning prestigious winner shirts and medals as a testament to their mathematical prowess and teamwork.
